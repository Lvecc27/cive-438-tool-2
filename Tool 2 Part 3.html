Percolation Estimator

A simplified annual water balance model for single-layer tailings cover design. Estimates percolation through the cover using the SCS Curve Number method and Darcy's Law.

Model Equations
S = (25400 / CN) − 254  [SCS retention, mm]
Q = (P − 0.2·S)² / (P + 0.8·S)  [Runoff, mm/yr]
Infiltration = P − Q
ΔW = max(0, Infiltration − ET)  [Water available]
q_darcy = K · i  [Darcy flux, mm/yr]
Percolation = min(ΔW, q_darcy)  [Limiting term]

Percolation is limited by whichever is smaller: the water available after ET, or the Darcy flux capacity of the cover. A low-K cover may be capacity-limited even when water is available; a high-K cover passes water freely up to supply limits.

Assumptions & Caveats
  • Annual lumped water balance — does not capture seasonal or storm-event dynamics
  • SCS-CN applied annually; method was developed for storm events and may overestimate runoff
  • ET assumed fully available (no soil moisture accounting); real ET may be water-limited
  • Single homogeneous cover layer; no preferential flow, macropores, or freeze-thaw effects
  • Hydraulic gradient assumed constant; real gradient varies with saturation state
  • Does not account for lateral drainage or geomembrane components
  • Results should be verified against site-specific data and regulatory guidance (e.g., EPA HELP model)
